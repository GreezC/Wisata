Gallery in Morocco

Chefchaouen

Chefchaouen is a small town known for its distinctive blue-painted architecture, giving it a calm and peaceful feel. The town is situated at an altitude of around 600 meters above sea level and is surrounded by beautiful mountains.

Kasbah des Oudaias

Known for its charming architecture and peaceful atmosphere, this kasbah is an excellent example of traditional Moroccan military and residential architecture.

Forêt de Cèdres d'Azrou

This cedar forest is one of the largest and most important forests in Morocco. Located at an altitude of about 1,200 to 1,800 meters above sea level, the forest is surrounded by stunning mountain views and offers a cool and fresh environment.

Hassan Mosque

The Hassan Mosque is an unfinished mosque and is one of the most important historical sites in Rabat. Its construction began in 1195 by Sultan Yacoub al-Mansour of the Almohad dynasty with the aim of making it one of the largest and most beautiful mosques in the Islamic world.

Chellah

Chellah is a historical site that includes the ruins of a Roman city and an Islamic cemetery. Situated on the banks of the Bou Regreg River, the site offers a fascinating look at the layered history and architecture of various periods.

Jemaa el Fnaa

Jemaa el-Fnaa is the bustling social and commercial center of Marrakech. This square is the heart of the city and a gathering place for locals and tourists alike. Known for its vibrant atmosphere, Jemaa el-Fnaa offers a colorful and diverse experience.

Akchour

Akchour is a mountainous area known for its natural beauty, including stunning waterfalls, natural bridges, and spectacular mountain views. It is a popular destination for nature lovers and travelers seeking a calming and immersive outdoor experience.

Tangier

Tangier is a strategic port city and a major gateway between Europe and Africa. Situated at the western end of the Mediterranean Sea, Tangier has a long and rich history and is an important cultural meeting point.

Ouzoud Waterfall

Ouzoud Waterfall (or Cascades d'Ouzoud) is a waterfall located in the Middle Atlas Mountains, near the village of Tanaghmeilt, Morocco. The waterfall is known for its spectacular beauty and power of flow, with a height of about 110 meters. It is one of the highest waterfalls in Morocco and is a popular place for nature tourism.

Reservoir Al Wahda

Al Wahda Reservoir is one of the largest reservoirs in Morocco. Also known as Al Wahda Dam, it was built in the 1950s to control the flow of the Oum Er-Rbia River and provide water for irrigation as well as domestic and industrial needs.

Toubkal

Mount Toubkal stands at about 4,167 metres (13,671 ft) above sea level, making it the highest peak in Morocco and North Africa. Located in the Toubkal National Park, it is a popular destination for climbers and nature lovers.

Medina Of Fes

One of the best-preserved medinas in Morocco and a UNESCO World Heritage Site, it is renowned for its ancient architecture, winding streets and bustling markets.
